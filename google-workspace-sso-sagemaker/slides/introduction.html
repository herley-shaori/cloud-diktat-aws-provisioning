Chapter 2: The Traditional Way (And Why It Fails)

Understanding the problem before we solve it

Before SSO, companies would create separate AWS IAM users for each employee. Let us examine why this approach creates challenges:

üîë

Multiple Passwords

The Data Scientist already has a Google password. Now they need another password for AWS? Studies show users with multiple passwords often reuse them or write them down, both security risks.

Offboarding Risk

When someone leaves the company, IT must remember to disable their AWS account separately from their Google account. Miss one, and you have a security breach waiting to happen.

üêå

Slow Onboarding

The Data Scientist cannot start working until IT manually creates their AWS account. This could take days, and multiply that by every new hire across the organization.

üìã

Audit Complexity

During security audits, you need to verify that your AWS users match your actual employees. With separate identity systems, this becomes a spreadsheet nightmare.

Key Concept: The root problem is maintaining two separate identity systems. The solution is to make one system the "source of truth" for identity.

Chapter 3: Enter Single Sign-On

One identity to rule them all

Single Sign-On (SSO) allows users to authenticate once with their primary identity provider (in our case, Google Workspace) and gain access to multiple applications (like AWS) without logging in again.

Think of it like a hotel key card: you check in once at the front desk, and that same card opens your room, the gym, and the pool. You do not need a separate key for each door.

Chapter 5: What We Learned

Key takeaways from this tutorial

Identity Federation

We learned that identity federation allows one system (Google Workspace) to vouch for a user's identity to another system (AWS). This eliminates duplicate accounts and passwords.

SAML Protocol

SAML (Security Assertion Markup Language) is the protocol that makes this possible. It is a standard way for identity providers to communicate authentication and authorization data.

SCIM Provisioning

SCIM automatically syncs users and groups from Google to AWS. When a Data Scientist is added to Google Workspace, SCIM creates their identity in AWS IAM Identity Center.

Permission Sets

AWS Permission Sets define what users can do. By assigning the DataScientist permission set to the DataScientists group, all members automatically get the right access.
